# Market-Ready Notes

## Tool name

**The Home-energy check-up (Australia)**

Developed by **Tech Innovation Experts**.

## Intended use

This tool is designed as a public-facing educational and decision-support prototype for Australian households. It helps users complete a short home-energy check-up, identify common energy-wasting features, and receive a practical action plan.

The tool is suitable for:

- general household energy education;
- community engagement activities;
- early-stage home energy awareness programs;
- demonstration of behaviour-focused energy advice;
- research-informed public outreach.

## What the tool is

The Home-energy check-up (Australia) is a lightweight web-based application that provides practical guidance on:

- heating and cooling settings;
- hot-water and shower behaviour;
- lighting upgrades;
- draught sealing;
- curtain and blind use;
- standby appliances;
- ceiling or roof insulation awareness;
- renter- and owner-appropriate action planning.

The interface is designed to feel like a short home check-up rather than a long survey.

## What the tool is not

This tool is **not**:

- a certified home energy assessment;
- an accredited NatHERS rating tool;
- an NCC compliance assessment tool;
- a substitute for professional building, electrical, plumbing, or energy advice;
- a solar, battery, tariff, or appliance sizing calculator;
- a validated financial savings calculator.

All dollar savings estimates should be treated as **indicative only** unless validated with local tariffs, household behaviour, climate zone, appliance performance, and actual consumption data.

## Market-positioning statement

Recommended wording for public use:

> The Home-energy check-up (Australia) is a free educational tool that helps households identify common energy-wasting features and build a practical action plan. It provides indicative guidance only and does not replace professional energy assessment, accredited NatHERS rating, or NCC compliance advice.

## NCC and NatHERS wording

Use this safer wording:

> This tool is informed by Australian home energy-efficiency guidance and aligned with general NCC 2022 / NatHERS Whole of Home concepts. It is not a certified NCC compliance tool or accredited NatHERS rating assessment.

Avoid writing:

> This tool complies with NCC.

That claim is too strong unless the application has been formally validated and accepted for compliance use.

## Certificate wording

The certificate should remain a recognition certificate only.

Recommended wording:

> Recognition certificate only. Not a certified energy assessment or accredited NCC/NatHERS rating.

Do not describe the certificate as a qualification, licence, professional credential, or formal competency award.

## Recommended public disclaimer

> This tool provides general educational guidance for household energy-saving decisions. The recommendations and estimated savings are indicative and may vary depending on climate, tariff, appliance efficiency, household behaviour, building condition, and occupancy. Users should seek professional advice for major upgrades, electrical work, plumbing work, building compliance, rental disputes, or investment decisions.

## Data and privacy position

For a simple public prototype, the safest position is:

- do not collect unnecessary personal information;
- do not store user names, emails, addresses, or bills unless essential;
- do not collect exact home addresses in the first version;
- avoid asking for income, health, ethnicity, or other sensitive information;
- allow users to download their result without requiring account creation.

If analytics are added later, include a privacy notice.

## Development priorities before wider release

Before public dissemination, the following improvements are recommended:

1. Validate indicative savings assumptions using Australian tariff and consumption scenarios.
2. Add state or climate-zone adjustment for recommendations.
3. Add a short privacy statement in the app footer.
4. Test usability with at least 10–20 target users.
5. Check mobile responsiveness across major screen sizes.
6. Review all wording for consumer-law risk, especially savings claims.
7. Add accessibility improvements, including keyboard navigation and alt text.
8. Keep the workflow short to avoid survey fatigue.
