# Content Evidence Notes

This file records the evidence basis behind the first public prototype. Keep citations in documentation, not inside every app screen.

## Heating and cooling

Australian Government household guidance recommends heating around 18–20°C in winter and cooling around 25–27°C in summer. It also states that each degree of additional heating or cooling can increase energy use by about 5–10%.

Prototype use:

- Thermostat hotspot
- Heating/cooling recommendation
- Indicative thermostat savings range

## Hot water

Australian Government guidance identifies water heating as one of the largest household energy uses, commonly around 15–30% of household energy use.

Prototype use:

- Shower time question
- Water-efficient showerhead recommendation
- Indicative shower-time savings

## Lighting

Australian Government guidance states that LEDs use about 75% less energy than halogen bulbs and last longer.

Prototype use:

- Old light-bulb hotspot
- LED replacement recommendation
- Indicative LED savings

## Appliances and standby

Australian Government guidance notes that appliances can account for around 30% of home energy use. Smart power strips can reduce standby energy waste by cutting power to appliances not in use.

Prototype use:

- Standby appliances hotspot
- Smart power board recommendation
- Conservative standby savings estimate

## Building shell: curtains, draughts, and insulation

Australian household guidance supports practical measures such as installing drapes/blinds, adding insulation, and sealing/blocking draughts as part of reducing energy use and improving comfort.

Prototype use:

- Curtains/window hotspot
- Draught gap hotspot
- Ceiling/roof insulation hotspot
- Renter/owner-specific recommendations

## Why some actions are not monetised yet

The prototype does not assign dollar savings to draught sealing, curtains, insulation, or solar because these depend strongly on climate zone, building envelope condition, heating/cooling system, orientation, glazing, occupancy, installation quality, and tariffs.

This is a deliberate credibility choice. Monetising these actions too early would make the app look more precise than it is.
